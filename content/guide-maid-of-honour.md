# How to Write a Maid of Honour Speech

*By Adrian Simpson, professional speechwriter · Updated [Month Year]*

The maid of honour speech is one of the highlights of the modern wedding — funny, heartfelt, and full of the friendship only you two share. Here's exactly how to write one that has the room laughing and reaching for the tissues, with examples to adapt.

## How long should a maid of honour speech be?
**A maid of honour speech should last around four to five minutes — roughly 550 to 750 words. Tight and heartfelt beats long and rambling.**

You want two good stories and a genuine, moving finish. Time it out loud; if you're over six minutes, cut the weaker story rather than trimming everything evenly.

## How do you start a maid of honour speech?
**Open with a warm, funny line that establishes who you are and your friendship with the bride — a little gentle self-deprecation or mock-reluctance works beautifully.**

For example: "Hello, everyone — I'm Katie, the maid of honour, which is a fancy way of saying I'm the friend who has known Sarah long enough to have real material, and who loves her far too much to use most of it."

Get the room smiling early; it makes the heartfelt ending land all the harder.

## What should a maid of honour speech include?
**Include who you are, how you met the bride, one or two affectionate stories about her, a warm welcome to her partner, and a sincere toast — in that order.**

The structure mirrors the best man's, with its own warmth:
1. **Opening hook** and who you are.
2. **How you met** the bride and how long you've known her.
3. **One or two stories** that capture her — funny, fond, true.
4. **The turn** — how happy her partner makes her, and a warm welcome to them.
5. **A sincere word** about your friend.
6. **The toast.**

## How do you balance funny and sentimental?
**Front-load the humour and finish on heart. Tease your friend with affection early, then let the speech grow sincere as you build to the toast.**

The best maid of honour speeches earn their emotional ending by being genuinely funny first. Keep the teasing warm — you're celebrating her, not exposing her — and when you turn sincere, mean every word. Don't undercut the heartfelt finish with one last gag.

## Maid of honour speech examples
**Here's a short worked passage to adapt.**

> "Hi everyone, I'm Katie — Sarah's maid of honour, and her best friend since we were eleven and she defended me from a much scarier girl in the dinner queue. I've been paying her back in embarrassing speeches ever since.
>
> Over twenty years, Sarah has been my partner in every questionable haircut, every terrible date, and every two-in-the-morning heart-to-heart. She is the most loyal, generous, ferociously kind person I know — and the only person who'd help you hide a body and then make you a cup of tea afterwards.
>
> And then James came along. I'll be honest, I was prepared to dislike him on principle. I couldn't. Because I have never seen Sarah this happy — properly, calmly, completely happy. James, you see her exactly as the rest of us do, and you adore her for it. Thank you for that.
>
> Sarah, you are my favourite person in the world, and you deserve every bit of this. Everyone, please raise your glasses — to the bride and groom. To Sarah and James."

The shape: funny opening, warm character portrait, the turn, sincerity, toast.

## How do you end with a toast?
**Finish with one heartfelt sentence about your friend, then invite everyone to raise their glasses to the couple with a clear, simple toast.**

Drop the jokes for the landing. Say the true thing, pause, then "please raise your glasses — to the bride and groom" (or to the couple by name). Lift your glass clearly so the room follows.

## Mistakes to avoid
**Avoid oversharing private stories, mentioning exes, in-jokes only you two understand, and forgetting to welcome the bride's partner.**

- **Oversharing.** Keep the truly private things private — warmth, not exposure.
- **Exes.** Never. Same rule as the best man.
- **In-jokes.** If the room can't follow it, cut it.
- **Forgetting the partner.** A genuine welcome to them is part of the job.
- **Winging it.** Practise aloud — the emotional lines especially.

## Your maid of honour speech checklist
- [ ] Four to five minutes, timed aloud
- [ ] A funny, warm opening
- [ ] One or two affectionate, appropriate stories
- [ ] A genuine welcome to the bride's partner
- [ ] A sincere finish, then a clear toast
- [ ] Practised aloud, printed large
